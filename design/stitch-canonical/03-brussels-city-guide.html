BELGIUM · CITY GUIDE

Brussels

A crossroads of European sensibility where grand Flemish heritage meets the quiet luxury of contemporary Ixelles.

Cities › Brussels
23 companions in Brussels

Brussels reads quietly for a capital. Beneath the city's official polish — the EU institutions, the diplomatic quarters, the carefully framed Grand Place — runs a more candid Brussels: the late-night light spilling from Saint-Gilles cafés, the slow conversations of the Marolles, the considered social rhythms of Ixelles. It's a city that rewards companions who can move between its registers — French at lunch, Dutch in the evening, English when it serves — and who treat discretion as a kind of fluency.

The companions who choose to live and work in Brussels tend to share a certain cosmopolitanism. Many are multilingual; many have travelled. Many have made the city their home not for its scale but for its restraint. Brussels offers the European sensibility that other capitals only impersonate: a preference for quiet rooms, considered company, and evenings that don't need explaining the next morning.

Our verified independent providers in Brussels span the city's most-loved neighbourhoods — Ixelles and Saint-Gilles for the bohemian, Uccle and Woluwe for the residential calm, the Centre and Sablon for the civic and the storied. Filter by language, by availability, or by neighbourhood. Or read on, and explore the city as we see it.
